[1] Obama opened on a historic note, echoing John F Kennedy’s phrase that the executive and the legislative branches are "partners for progress" [2] Obama said that "there is much progress to report", citing the end of the war in Iraq, a rebounding economy and a retreat from the crises of the past [3] He began his policy prescriptions by claiming that bolstering the middle class was key for economic growth [4] Equality of opportunity was a notable theme from his opening remarks, arguing that government must work "on behalf of the many, and not just the few" [5] Obama proceeded to prod Congress on the need to foster bipartisanship and put the interests of the nation before party - in anticipation of his remarks on the budget and deficit [6] Praising efforts that have reduced the deficit by $2 [7] 5trillion, Obama denounced the "sequester", and any potential solution privileging military spending over discretionary and entitlement spending [8] Obama conceded that the biggest driver of long-term debt was the cost of healthcare for the elderly and called for modest reforms that leave seniors with onerous sacrifices [9] The president’s agenda was one of fairness, asking all quarters of American society to absorb the costs of deficit reduction [10] On Medicare, the president pledged to enact reforms that would mirror the savings proposed by the Simpson-Bowles commission - by reducing taxpayer subsidies to prescription drug companies, asking more from wealthy seniors and tying the price of healthcare to measures of quality [11] Arguing for increased scrutiny in the tax code, Obama echoed the theme of fairness, implying growth and fairness are interconnected [12] He proposed, appealing to both parties, a simpler tax code which would not contain loopholes for the wealthy and would create incentives for companies creating jobs in America [13] "The greatest nation on Earth cannot keep conducting its business by drifting from one manufactured crisis to the next [14] " However, it is a growing economy, and not simply deficit reduction that should be the aim of the United States, argued Obama [15] The three policy proposals for higher levels of growth were attracting more jobs, equipping individuals with the right skills and ensuring workers are paid fairly [16] Obama appealed to both parties by claiming that all proposals he laid out for increased growth were fully paid for and would not increase the deficit [17] To make America a magnet for new jobs and manufacturing, the President proposed fifteen new manufacturing hubs that would turn depressed regions into global centres of hi-tech jobs [18] "Now is not the time to gut these job-creating investments in science and innovation [19] Now is the time to reach a level of research and development not seen since the height of the Space Race [20] " Obama called for additional science and research spending and elaborated the threat of climate change, asking Congress to take on a market-based solution to the challenge it poses, and claiming that if they did not act, he would [21] He proposed using oil and gas revenues to create an Energy Security Trust that would free Americans from dependence on oil and increase innovation in the sphere of clean energy [22] He also laid out the goal to cut in half the energy wasted by homes and businesses in the next two decades through a partnership with the states [23] Obama criticised America’s aging infrastructure as a hindrance to growth, proposing a "Fix-it-First" program to carry out the most urgent repairs needed in the country, in addition to a "Partnership to Rebuild America" by seeking private capital to renew infrastructure [24] Obama argued that a bipartisan effort to help Americans to refinance their mortgages is critical and would be accepted by him immediately [25] The president pushed hard on the need to boost early education efforts by instituting a national preschool program, investments he claimed would mean future savings [26] Obama additionally asked Congress to change the Higher Education Act to make affordability and value parameters for federal aid, and announced the release of a scorecard ranking colleges by getting "the most bang for your educational buck" [27] "The time has come to pass comprehensive immigration reform [28] " Obama called for enhanced border security, pathways to citizenship and the rectification of the legal immigration framework as the keys to any comprehensive immigration reform bill [29] The President urged the House to pass the Domestic Violence Against Women Act, as well as asking Congress to pass the Paycheck Fairness Act [30] An additional policy recommendation was to raise the minimum wage [31] "Tonight, let’s declare that in the wealthiest nation on Earth, no one who works full-time should have to live in poverty, and raise the federal minimum wage to $9 [32] 00 an hour [33] " Obama announced that America will soon end its mission in Afghanistan and defeat the core of al-Qaeda [34] 34,000 troops will return this year, he said, and by the end of next year, the war will be over [35] The American commitment to Afghanistan would include training for Afghan forces, and counterterrorism efforts to pursue enemies of America [36] Obama then claimed that his administration has created a transparent and "durable" counter-terrorism policy, and will continue to engage Congress to ensure American counter-terrorism policies are consistent with the letter of the law [37] He stated that America would prevent Iran from getting a nuclear weapon and that it would take threats from cyber-attacks much more seriously [38] Obama said that the US will aim to eradicate extreme poverty and AIDS, and strive to be a beacon of the ideal of democracy and self-governance to citizens of all countries [39] To raucous applause, he said that the US would stand steadfast with Israel [40] He discussed the right to vote and the difficulty voting has taken on in recent times, announcing a non-partisan commission to improve voting conditions in America [41] "This time it’s different," said the President, on the topic of gun control, advocating common-sense reform such as background checks, gun resale limitations and controls on assault weapons and oversized magazine clips [42] "In the two months since Newtown, more than a thousand birthdays, graduations, and anniversaries have been stolen from our lives by a bullet from a gun [43] " Giving the example of fifteen-year-old Hadiya Pendleton, who, after performing at Obama’s inauguration, was shot dead in Chicago, he repeated the phrase, "they deserve a vote", to wild applause [44] He concluded his speech on the values of and the obligations to citizenship, giving the example of a number of citizens who demonstrate self-sacrifice and civic virtue [45] "We are citizens [46] It’s a word that doesn’t just describe our nationality or legal status [47] It describes the way we’re made [48] It describes what we believe [49] It captures the enduring idea that this country only works when we accept certain obligations to one another and to future generations; that our rights are wrapped up in the rights of others; and that well into our third century as a nation, it remains the task of us all, as citizens of these United States, to be the authors of the next great chapter in our American story [50] "
